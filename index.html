Kelly Quinn

PhD Candidate in Sociology, UC Berkeley

I am a PhD candidate in Sociology at the University of California, Berkeley, and a researcher at the UC Berkeley Labor Center. My work examines how labor market institutions like unions shape job quality, worker power, and economic security in service industries.

My dissertation consists of three papers that explore these dynamics from both quantitative and qualitative perspectives: a study of unions' role in securing retirement benefits across labor market segments; an analysis of a successful insourcing campaign by service workers at a large public university; and an examination of wage penalties associated with outsourced work. Together, this research highlights how employment structures and collective organization shape inequality in the labor market, using both quantitative and qualitative methods to better understand how labor market institutions can strengthen economic security for workers.
